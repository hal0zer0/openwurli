# Release Codename Reference

Codenames are short lyric snippets (2-4 words, 4 only if short) from songs
featuring the Wurlitzer electric piano. PascalCase, no spaces. Snippets must
NOT match the song title — they should be a distinctive phrase from the lyrics.

## Notes on Wurlitzer Models

Not all songs use the 200/200A that OpenWurli models, but all are part of the
Wurlitzer electric piano family and its cultural legacy.

- **EP-200/200A** (solid-state, our model): Supertramp, Pink Floyd, Queen,
  Steely Dan, ELO, Stealers Wheel, Foreigner, Daft Punk
- **140B/145B** (tube amp): The Doors, Aretha Franklin (Spooner Oldham),
  Cannonball Adderley (Joe Zawinul)
- **120** (tube, earliest): Ray Charles
- **G-Funk era**: mixed keyboard arsenals (Wurlitzer among Korg, Moog, Roland)

## Trivia

- Daft Punk used Supertramp's actual Wurlitzer on "Digital Love"
- Joe Zawinul's 140B on "Mercy, Mercy, Mercy" was previously owned by Ray Charles
- John Deacon wrote "You're My Best Friend" because Freddie Mercury refused to
  play the Wurlitzer — so Deacon took it home and learned piano on it
- Roger Hodgson bought his Wurlitzer at 19; it defined Supertramp's entire sound
- Joni Mitchell's "Woodstock" is solo Wurlitzer with tremolo — no other instruments
